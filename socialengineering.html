Social Engineering & Phishing

Learn the human side of security — how attackers manipulate people and how to defend against it.

Social engineering targets the human element. Study attack techniques (phishing, vishing, pretexting), psychology of persuasion, and practical defenses such as awareness programs, secure culture, and simulated phishing campaigns.

Tools & Platforms

Gophish, King Phisher, Social-Engineer Toolkit (SET), PhishTool, Mailslurper (testing), Browser DevTools (recon)

Resources

Open any resource in a new tab. Use tools in test labs or on consenting targets only — never misuse them.

What to Learn ✅

  • Principles of social engineering & persuasion (Cialdini's principles)
  • Types of phishing: email, spear-phishing, whaling
  • Vishing (voice phishing) and smishing (SMS phishing)
  • Pretexting and impersonation techniques
  • Reconnaissance: OSINT for social engineering
  • Building believable phishing pages (for simulations)
  • Running phishing simulations with GoPhish / King Phisher
  • Metrics & reporting: click rates, credential submissions, awareness KPIs
  • Defensive measures: email filtering, DMARC/DKIM/SPF, MFA, training
  • Legal & ethical constraints — red team rules of engagement

Suggested Practical Exercises

  • Set up a local GoPhish instance and create a safe test campaign (use mock targets you control).
  • Analyze a phishing sample: inspect HTML, resources, and infrastructure used.
  • Design an awareness micro-training and measure change in behavior after a simulated phish.
